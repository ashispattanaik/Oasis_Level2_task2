Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the "Father of the Nation," was an influential leader and freedom fighter in India's struggle for independence from British rule.

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India, Gandhi devoted his life to promoting non-violence, civil disobedience, and communal harmony.

He led various movements such as the Salt March, Quit India Movement, and the Non-Cooperation Movement, inspiring millions to participate in the fight for freedom.

Gandhi's philosophy of "Satyagraha" emphasized truth and non-violence as the most potent weapons for social and political change.

He lived a simple life, wearing traditional Indian clothes and advocating self-reliance and communal harmony.

Mahatma Gandhi's principles and teachings continue to inspire people worldwide in their pursuit of justice, peace, and equality.

Gandhi's message of truth, love, and compassion remains relevant, and his legacy will forever be etched in history.
